M7583*
(BTK inhibitor)

Proposed profile

  • M7583* is an investigational, ATP-competitive, irreversible BTK inhibitor.1 This
    molecule is currently under clinical investigation and has not been proven to be safe
    and effective
  • BTK is a key component of BCR signaling that regulates B-cell proliferation and
    survival2,3
  • The BCR pathway is implicated in the pathogenesis of B-cell malignancies such as
    diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL), mantle cell lymphoma (MCL), B- cell chronic
    lymphocytic leukemia (CLL), Waldenström macroglobulinemia (WM), and multiple
    myeloma (MM)3,4
  • M7583 is currently being investigated as potential therapy for various B-cell malignancies3
  • Development of and clinical/preclinical investigation of BTK inhibitors may provide
    additional insight into treatment of patient groups who have not shown significant
    response to other therapies3

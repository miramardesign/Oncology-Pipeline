M7583*
(BTK inhibitor)

Proposed profile

  • M7583* is an investigational, ATP-competitive, irreversible BTK inhibitor.1 This molecule is currently under clinical investigation and has not been proven to be safe and effective
  • BTK is a key component of BCR signaling that regulates B-cell proliferation and survival2,3
  • The BCR pathway is implicated in the pathogenesis of B-cell malignancies such as diffuse large B-cell lymphoma (DLBCL), mantle cell lymphoma (MCL), B- cell chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL), Waldenström macroglobulinemia (WM), and multiple myeloma (MM)3,4
  • M7583 is currently being investigated as potential therapy for various B-cell malignancies3
  • Development of and clinical/preclinical investigation of BTK inhibitors may provide additional insight into treatment of patient groups who have not shown significant response to other therapies3
BCR, B-cell antigen receptor; BTK, Bruton tyrosine kinase; elf4E, Eukaryotic translation initiation factor 4E; GSK-3, glycogen synthase kinase 3; LYN, tyrosine-protein kinase Lyn; mTOR, mammalian target of rapamycin;
NF-kβ, nuclear factor kappa-light-chain-enhancer of activated B cells; p70S6K, ribosomal protein S6 kinase beta-1; PI3K, phosphatidylinositide 3-kinase; PKC, protein kinase C; PLCγ2, phospholipase Cγ2; SYK, spleen tyrosine kinase.

*M7583 is under clinical investigation and has not been proven to be safe and effective. There is no guarantee M7583 will be approved in the sought-after indications. The relevance of these preclinical findings on the efficacy and safety in the
clinical setting has not been established.

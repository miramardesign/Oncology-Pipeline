M8891*
(MetAP2 inhibitor)

Proposed profile

  • M8891* is an investigational, small-molecule designed as a reversible, orally
    bioavailable inhibitor of Methionine aminopeptidase 2 (MetAP2). This molecule is
    currently under clinical investigation and has not been proven to be safe and effective
  • MetAP2 is an enzyme that is important for angiogenesis and proliferation in several
    tumor types; it therefore serves as a relevant therapeutic target for anti-cancer
    treatment development
Pipeline compounds are being investigated for the treatment of various diseases. Efficacy and safety of these compounds are still under investigation in various indications. Regulatory approval is dependent on the completion of
the study programs and review by local regulatory authorities, and varies from country to country. Clinical trial information is available at www.clinicaltrials.gov.

*M8891 (MetAP2i) is under clinical investigation and has not been proven to be safe and effective.
There is no guarantee M8891 will be approved in the sought-after indications. The relevance of these preclinical findings on the efficacy and safety in the clinical setting has not been established.

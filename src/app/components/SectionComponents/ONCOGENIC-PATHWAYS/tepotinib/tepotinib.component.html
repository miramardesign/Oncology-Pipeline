Tepotinib*
(MET kinase inhibitor)

Proposed profile

  • Tepotinib is an investigational oral MET inhibitor.1,2 This molecule is currently under clinical investigation and has not been proven to be safe and effective
  • MET activation is a key driver of tumor progression and therapy resistance3
  • Two different roles for MET in tumor biology make it a plausible target:

─ MET can act as driver of tumorigenesis4-7

  • Some tumors depend on MET signaling for their growth, represented by MET pathway aberrations (MET can act as MET amplification, MET activating mutations – such as MET exon 14 skipping, MET and/or HGF overexpression)

─ MET can act as co-stimulatory or resistance mediator4-8

  • Disruption of tumor growth may be achieved by interfering with cross-talking pathways between MET and other RTKs, to block different tumor-specific pathways in parallel
RTKs – receptor Tyrosine Kinases

Bintrafusp alfa*
(TGF-β trap/anti–PD-L1)

Proposed profile

  • Bintrafusp alfa (M7824) is an innovative bi-functional fusion protein1 composed of
    the extracellular domain of human TGF-β receptor II (a TGF-β “trap”) fused to a fully
    human IgG1 mAb blocking PD-L1
  • This molecule is currently under clinical investigation and has not been proven to be
    safe and effective
  • Evasion of antitumor immunity is a hallmark of cancer2
    ─ TGF-β is a regulatory cytokine with diverse effects in the immune system that
    maintains immunologic self-tolerance3-5
    ─ PD-1 and PD-L1 dampen T-cell activity and can be co-opted by tumors to suppress
    T-cell–mediated antitumor immune responses6
  • The TGF-β and PD-L1 pathways are 2 key tumor immune evasion pathways that are thought to have independent and complementary immunosuppressive functions
*Bintrafusp alfa (M7824) is under clinical investigation and has not been proven to be safe and effective. There is no guarantee M7824 will be approved in the sought-after indications. The relevance of these preclinical findings
on the efficacy and safety in the clinical setting has not been established.

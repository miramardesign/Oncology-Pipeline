Proposed profile

Mechanisms of IL-12 as a tumor targeting therapy1

  • IL-12 is a potent proinflammatory cytokine produced by dendritic cells
    and macrophages that is a key mediator of the transition from innate to adaptive immunity1-4
  • IL-12 is thought to induce differentiation of naive CD4+ T cells to the Th1 phenotype5
  • IL-12 is thought to increase the proliferation and lytic capacity of CD8+ cytotoxic T cells and NK cells6-10
  • IL-12 is thought to promote IFN-γ production via NK cells and T cells11
    ─ Increases the production of IP-10 and MIG, which then mediate an antiangiogenic
    effect12,13
    ─ Enhances antigen presentation through paracrine upregulation of MHC class I and
    II expression14,15
APC, antigen-presenting cell; IP-10, interferon-inducible protein 10; MIG, monokine induced by interferon γ; NK, natural killer cell; Tc, cytotoxic T lymphocyte; Th, T helper lymphocyte.
